School story — ~2 minutes voiceover (about 240–280 words at a calm pace)
Title on screen: “The Sleeper Car in the Back Lot”

--- HOOK ~0:00–0:20 ---
Every high school parking lot has that one absolute junker. At Crestview High, it was Ethan’s ride—a dented, faded 1998 sedan with peeling paint and a mismatched passenger door. People openly laughed when it rattled into the student lot. Ethan just smiled. They thought he couldn't afford better. They had no idea what was hiding under the hood.

--- BEAT 1 ~0:20–0:45 ---
Fact one: real speed doesn't need to show off. Ethan spent every single weekend covered in grease in his grandfather's garage. While other kids bought flashy stickers and loud exhaust tips for their stock hatchbacks, Ethan was quietly dropping a twin-turbocharged V8 engine into a chassis that looked like it belonged in a scrapyard.

--- BEAT 2 ~0:45–1:10 ---
Fact two: overconfidence is a quick way to lose. Kyle, the richest kid in the senior class, drove a brand-new, customized sports car his parents bought him. He spent weeks mocking Ethan’s ride, calling it a motorized trash can. Ethan never argued. He just waited for the annual charity track day at the local drag strip.

--- BEAT 3 ~1:10–1:35 ---
Fact three: the stopwatch never lies. When Kyle challenged Ethan to a straight-line race, a massive crowd gathered, expecting a total blowout. They got one—just not the way they thought. The light turned green, Kyle’s sports car roared, but Ethan’s junker vanished. It launched so hard the front tires almost left the asphalt, leaving a hundred-thousand-dollar sports car in a cloud of dust.

--- BEAT 4 ~1:35–1:55 ---
Fact four: true car enthusiasts respect the build, not the badge. By Monday morning, the video had gone completely viral across the entire county. Kyle wouldn't even park in the main lot anymore. Ethan, however, didn't boast. He just parked in his usual distant spot, threw on his backpack, and walked to class like nothing happened.

--- CLOSER ~1:55–2:10 ---
Fact five: never judge a machine by its rust. The car community calls it a "sleeper"—a vehicle that looks entirely harmless but packs devastating performance. And Crestview High learned a permanent lesson: sometimes the quietest thing in the parking lot is just waiting for the right moment to leave everyone else far behind.

--- END ---
